Cryptographic Chaining

Each record contains a hash of the previous block, creating a mathematical link that spans the entire history.

Immutable Records

Once committed, data cannot be edited or deleted without breaking the chain, rendering the ledger append-only.

Alteration Prevention

Changing a single bit of data changes the resulting hash, immediately alerting the system to unauthorized tampering.

Absolute Integrity

Trust is shifted from a central authority to the immutable laws of mathematics and distributed verification.
