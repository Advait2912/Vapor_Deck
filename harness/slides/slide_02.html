Foundations of Blockchain Architecture

Data Integrity

Immutable Ledgers

Cryptographic hashing ensures that once data is written to a block, it cannot be altered without changing every subsequent block. This creates a verifiable, permanent history of transactions.

Core: SHA-256 • Merkle Trees • Chain-of-Trust

Network Layer

P2P Networking

Distributed nodes maintain the ledger without a central authority. Gossiping protocols ensure state synchronization across the globe with high fault tolerance.

Core: Node Discovery • Gossip Protocols • Decentralization

Consensus Evolution

The PoW to PoS Shift

Moving from computationally expensive Proof-of-Work (mining) to Proof-of-Stake (validators) drastically reduces the carbon footprint and energy requirements.

Impact: >99% energy reduction • Higher Scalability • Sustainable Growth
