Foundations of Machine Learning

Learning Paradigms
  • Supervised: Learning from labeled data to predict outcomes.
  • Unsupervised: Identifying hidden patterns in unlabeled data.
  • Semi-Supervised: Combining small labeled sets with large unlabeled pools.
Neural Architectures
  • Neural Networks: Mimicking biological neurons to process information.
  • Deep Learning: Using multi-layered networks for complex feature extraction.
  • Weights & Biases: Mathematical parameters adjusted during training.
The Fuel: Big Data — The efficacy of deep learning is directly proportional to the volume and quality of data available for model training, enabling the transition from rule-based systems to pattern-recognition intelligence.
